(Built-in data structures in Python)

List - a mutable array (can be altered after being instantiated)
Ex: an ordered collection of numbers [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]

Tuple - an immutable array (may not be altered after being instantiated)
  - Tuples store related data
Ex: coordinate (5, 1)

Dictionaries - store key-value pairs; referencing a key retrieves the value
Ex: {‘Jane’: 25, ‘John’: 27, ‘Adam’: 24’}

Sets - store a collection of unique items in an unordered manner
Ex: {‘dog’, ‘cat’, ‘fish’}

Deque (‘deck’; double ended queue) - allows you to insert or remove data from both ends
Ex: d = deque([1, 2, 3])
